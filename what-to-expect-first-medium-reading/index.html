    What to Expect in Your First Medium Reading

    Aileen McNamara · March 12, 2026

    If you have never had a medium reading before, you are probably feeling a mixture of hope, curiosity, and perhaps a little nervousness. This is entirely natural. A medium reading is a deeply personal experience, and knowing what to expect can help you feel more at ease and get the most from your session.

    Before the Reading Begins

    In the minutes before your session, take a quiet moment to centre yourself. You do not need to meditate or perform any ritual — simply take a few calm breaths and think gently about your loved one. If you have a specific person you hope to connect with, hold them in your thoughts. If you are open to whoever comes through, that intention is equally valid.

    You may bring a photograph or a personal item belonging to your loved one if it gives you comfort, but this is not required. The medium connects through energy and intention, not physical objects.

    The Opening Moments

    Your medium will begin by briefly explaining how they work and what you can expect. Different mediums receive information in different ways — some see images, others hear words, and some simply know information. Your medium will explain their primary channels so you understand how evidence will be presented.

    They will then open their awareness to the spirit world. This is not dramatic or theatrical. It is a quiet, focused shift of attention. The medium becomes receptive to communication from those on the other side.

    Receiving Evidence

    The first priority is evidence — verifiable information that confirms who is communicating. Your medium may describe physical features, personality characteristics, specific memories, names, or significant details about the person’s life or passing.

    Your role during this phase is to confirm or deny what is presented. Honest responses help the medium calibrate the connection. A simple yes or no is often sufficient. Try not to provide additional information unless asked — this helps ensure the evidence is genuinely coming from the spirit and not from your own words.

    It is worth noting that evidence may not always be immediately recognisable. Sometimes a detail makes sense only after the reading when you have time to reflect or speak with other family members. If something does not resonate immediately, make a note of it rather than dismissing it.

    The Messages

    Once identity is established — and you feel confident about who is communicating — messages begin to flow. These vary enormously from reading to reading. Some spirits deliver specific, detailed messages about your current life. Others express love in simpler terms. Some bring humour, exactly as they did in life. Others are more serious or reflective.

    The most meaningful aspect of the messages is usually not the words themselves but the feeling behind them. Many people describe a sense of the person’s presence — their energy, their manner, their unique way of expressing care. This felt connection is often what brings the deepest comfort.

    Asking Questions

    Most mediums welcome questions during or after the evidence-and-message phase. You can ask your loved one about specific topics, seek guidance, or request confirmation about something specific. The medium relays your question and shares whatever response comes through.

    Keep in mind that the answers may not be what you expected. The spirit world communicates in its own way, and responses can be symbolic, indirect, or surprising. Trust the process and remain open.

    After the Reading

    When the session ends, give yourself time. Do not rush back into activity. Many people find that the full impact of a reading unfolds over hours or days. Details that seemed minor during the session may suddenly carry profound significance. Emotions that were held in check may surface later.

    Write down what you remember. Key pieces of evidence, specific messages, and feelings that arose — all of this is worth recording while it is fresh. Some people find that journaling after a reading accelerates their healing process.

    What If It Did Not Go As Expected?

    Not every reading unfolds perfectly. Sometimes the connection is strong but the person you hoped for does not come through. Sometimes the evidence is abundant but the messages are brief. This does not mean the reading failed — it simply reflects the reality that spirit communication is a dynamic, unpredictable process.

    If your first reading did not fully meet your expectations, give it time. Reflect on what was received rather than what was absent. And know that subsequent readings often build on the connection established in the first.

    A Final Thought

    Your first medium reading is likely to be unlike any other experience you have had. It can be emotional, surprising, comforting, and transformative. Approach it with an open heart, realistic expectations, and the knowledge that whatever comes through is a gift of continuing love.
